Myth: Autistic adults cannot succeed professionally.

Truth: Autism does not prevent individuals from thriving in the workforce, yet the lack of proper support and accommodations has severely limited employment opportunities. According to the Autism Society, up to 85% of autistic adults with a college degree are unemployed or underemployed. A 2021 study published in the National Library of Medicine similarly found an unemployment rate of around 40% among adults with autism. The Autism Society also reports that autistic adults typically earn about 40% less than peers with other disabilities, and the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics notes that only 19.3% of people with disabilities were employed in 2020. These disparities stem from barriers such as interview difficulties, social and sensory challenges, and a lack of workplace accommodationss. Moreover, only about 30% of autistic employees disclose their diagnosis to employers, often fearing discrimination.
